    BCC Connect

    How to use this workspace

    A walk-through of every module — what each one does, how the data flows between them, and the non-obvious things to know. This page is kept current with the live app, including notifications, the full QuickBooks integration, automatic Outlook, private chat channels, and per-app permissions.

    Getting started

    A few things to do the first time you sign in.

    1. Sign in with your @bluecollarcoach.us Microsoft 365 account. The whole workspace is gated by your tenant — there's no separate password to remember.
    2. Click the 🔔 bell in the top bar → Enable alerts on this device, and accept the browser prompt. That's what lets reminders reach you even when the app is closed.
    3. Admins: open Admin → Users to promote teammates, mark people inactive, or tune per-app permissions (the ⚙ gear on each row). The first signed-in admin is bootstrapped automatically.
    4. Admins: shape the dropdown that powers CRM and Engagements at Admin → Customer Types, and drop connector credentials in at Admin → Integrations (QuickBooks, Google Ads, Meta, LinkedIn, Mailchimp, …). Click Test connection on each.
    5. Admins: set your company info at Admin → Company Info — name, phone, email, address, tagline. This drives the letterhead on the rate sheet and other customer-facing artifacts.
    Outlook just works: calendar sync and email-send are wired tenant-wide via Microsoft Graph — there is no per-person "Connect Outlook" step. See Microsoft 365 integration.

    My Day

    Your personal landing each morning.

    Greeting + today's date + a row of KPI chips (sessions today, sessions tomorrow, total contacts). On the right: a Clock in / Clock out timer that persists across page reloads. Three cards underneath show today's sessions, tomorrow's sessions, and quick links to the things you start most often.

    At the bottom is your daily log: Wins, Blockers, Tomorrow's plan. Save once a day; the most recent saved time shows next to the heading.

    Sessions

    Coaching calendar, focused on the week ahead, two-way with Outlook.

    Seven columns, one per day. Click a session card to edit; click + New session to create one. Sessions can link to a CRM contact (dropdown) so the same person shows up across modules.

    Outlook calendar sync (live)

    • Create / edit / delete a session and it automatically mirrors to your Outlook calendar — no setup, no "Connect" button.
    • Pull from Outlook brings your existing calendar events into the week so nothing is double-booked. Events sourced from Outlook show a blue left border; local-only ones show gold.
    Reminders: every session fires a notification 24 hours and 15 minutes before it starts. If a session has an assigned coach, only that coach is reminded. See Notifications.
    Cross-references: Sessions appear on My Day's today/tomorrow lists and the Scheduler month/year views. Same data, different lens.

    Scheduler

    Month and year overview.

    Toggle between month grid (clickable days that open a per-day modal with every session + event) and a 12-month year view (each month is a mini calendar; days with events are gold-tinted). Click any mini month to drill in.

    Read-only view — to add a session, the toolbar's + Session button jumps to Sessions with the chosen day pre-selected.

    Client Dashboard

    A one-page strategic snapshot for each client.

    Pick a client (a CRM company) from the dropdown and the page opens that client's dashboard — a grid of coaching cards: Vision, Core Values, Why, 90-Day Plan, SWOT, Bottleneck, Financials, KPIs, Meeting Pulse, People & Accountability, Marketing Plan, Flavor of Lean, SNE, and Books reading. Everything you type autosaves per client.

    Financials from QuickBooks

    Link a connected QuickBooks company in the toolbar and click ↻ Pull financials to fill Current ratio, Months of cash on hand, and Net margin straight from QBO. You can also type them by hand.

    Print: Ctrl/Cmd+P gives you a clean one-page handout of the whole dashboard for a client meeting.

    Team Chat

    Internal channels, with private membership.

    Pick a channel on the left; type at the bottom. Enter sends, Shift+Enter makes a new line. You can attach a photo with the 📎 button. Messages show the author, avatar, and time, grouped by day.

    Channels & membership

    • The default channels (general, coaching, bookkeeping, consulting, marketing, training, technology, ops) are open to everyone.
    • + New channel creates a private channel — visible only to its members. You're added as the first member automatically and the Members manager opens so you can add people.
    • The channel creator (and any admin) sees a 👥 Members button in the channel header. Use it to add/remove people, or flip the channel to “Open to everyone.” Private channels are hidden from non-members.
    • New-message alerts: if you're not on the Chat page, a new message in a channel you can see raises a notification. (You're never pinged for your own messages.)
    Moderation: you can delete your own messages; admins can delete any message or Clear channel. Deletions are recorded in the Activity Log.

    Notifications

    A bell in the top bar, plus reminders that reach you when the app is closed.

    The 🔔 bell shows an unread badge and opens a panel of recent notifications (Mark all read / Clear). Notifications are per-person and per-device — they're not shared across the team.

    What raises a notification

    • Event & session reminders — once at 24 hours out and once at 15 minutes out.
    • New chat messages in channels you belong to (when you're not already on the Chat page).
    • Deal / engagement changes — admins only: when anyone creates, edits, or moves an engagement card, every other admin is notified.
    • Feedback replies — when a piece of feedback you submitted is marked resolved, you're notified that it was addressed.

    Feedback

    The 💬 Feedback button in the top bar (also in the hamburger menu) lets anyone send a note, bug report, or idea — with an optional star rating. It records the page you were on. Submissions land in Admin → Feedback; when one is marked Resolved, the person who sent it gets a notification that it's been addressed.

    Where they show up

    • In the bell panel (always) and as a toast while a tab is open.
    • As a desktop / phone pop-up when a tab is in the background — once you've granted permission.
    • Even with the app fully closed for the time-based event/session reminders — these are sent from the server via Web Push.
    One-time setup per device: click the bell → Enable alerts on this device and accept the browser prompt. Until then you'll still see the in-app bell and toasts whenever a tab is open.

    CRM

    Contacts, companies, and the conversation history with each.

    List view

    Search by name, email, company, or tag. Filter by customer type (admin-driven), stage (lead/qualified/customer/inactive), or region (auto-populated from contact state codes). Click + New contact to add inline; click any row to open the detail view.

    Detail view

    Three sections side-by-side:

    • Profile: every field is inline-editable. Click Save changes to persist. Stage and customer type save on change (no button needed).
    • Needs: a checkable list. Strike through when met. Add inline; delete inline.
    • Conversations: a timeline of logged interactions (Note / Call / Email / Session / Meeting). Type a one-line summary + optional details; the entry is timestamped and attributed to whoever logged it.
    Print: the detail page has a clean print mode — hits Ctrl/Cmd+P to produce a contact handoff sheet for a colleague.

    Engagements

    Kanban pipeline of coaching/consulting deals.

    Six columns: New → Scheduled → Active → Waiting → Invoiced → Done. Drag a card between columns to move stage; each drag fires an audit row so you can see who moved what when.

    Each card: title, linked contact name, value, renewal/close date. Click to open the modal and edit. Service type is pulled from the Admin → Customer Types list so the same vocabulary works across CRM and Engagements.

    Admin alerts: whenever anyone creates, edits, or drags an engagement, every other admin gets a notification (the person who made the change isn't pinged). See Notifications.

    Rate Sheet

    Your coaching packages as a printable artifact + e-signature capture.

    The page renders a real letterhead with your company info (set on Admin → Company Info). Each package is edited inline — name, price, cadence (monthly / quarterly / annual / one-time), blurb. Add a package with the dashed "+ Add a package" button.

    The signature block at the bottom lets a customer draw on a canvas (mouse or finger). Save snapshots their name + linked contact + the current package list + the signature PNG into a bcc-rate-signature-* doc with timestamp + user-agent.

    Print: Ctrl/Cmd+P drops the toolbar/controls and gives you a clean letterhead for handoff. The signature block prints too, so a signed contract is one page.

    Documents

    Contracts, SOPs, customer files. Backed by Azure Blob.

    Drag-and-drop a file (or click to pick). Optionally set folder + tags. Up to 25 MB per upload. Files go to a Blob container scoped to your tenant; metadata stays in Cosmos so you can search by name, folder, or tag without downloading everything.

    Downloads happen via short-lived (15 min) signed URLs — no public link ever leaves the tenant.

    Marketing

    Track campaigns across Google Ads, Meta, LinkedIn, Mailchimp, WordPress, GoDaddy.

    The strip at the top mirrors your Admin → Integrations status per channel. Green dot = creds saved + last test succeeded. Configure first, then come here.

    Each campaign: name, channel, status (draft/active/paused/completed), budget + spend in USD, start/end dates, impressions/clicks/leads, free-form notes. Search + filter by channel or status.

    Bookkeeping

    A complete per-client bookkeeping workspace on top of live QuickBooks Online — everything for a client in one place, no extra navigation.

    The main page (your work queue)

    Open Bookkeeping from the home screen. You land on your work queue: the clients you can access, each with a 🔔 count of open to-dos (overdue flagged). Click a client to open its workspace. The Bookkeeping wordmark (top bar) and the page title are clickable from anywhere inside — they always take you back to this work queue.

    Inside a client

    Opening a client lands on its Overview (quick actions + open to-dos), not a wall of numbers. Use the ☰ menu (top-left of the workspace) to slide between sections, and ← Clients to return to the queue:

    • Overview — quick actions and the client's open to-dos, plus a recent-activity strip.
    • Activity — a per-user feed of what changed for this client (new transactions, files, emails, tasks, etc.) over the last 7–90 days, with a "Just me" filter.
    • New & transactions — create an invoice, an expense / purchase (already paid — cash or card), a bill (unpaid, A/P), a customer payment, or a journal entry (adjusting / reclass — debit/credit lines that must balance) straight into the client's QuickBooks, and Edit existing ones; add + Customer / + Vendor / + Item / + Account on the spot. Every post shows a confirm dialog (it writes to live books) and is logged. Browse Transactions / Payments / Expenses / All Invoices / All Bills. 📷 Scan receipt (AI) photographs or uploads a receipt and auto-fills the right transaction (vendor, date, line items, total), matching the vendor in QuickBooks when it can — and it keeps the sales tax as its own line so the posted total always matches the receipt.
    • Reports & KPIs — financial-health tiles (months/days of cash, current ratio, working capital, revenue/COGS/OpEx/net, margin) plus live financial statements — P&L, Balance Sheet, Trial Balance, General Ledger, Statement of Cash Flows — A/R & A/P aging (summary & detail), Customer / Vendor balances, Sales by Customer / by Product, and Invoice/Bill lists, for the period and Cash/Accrual method you pick. Sync refreshes stored monthly snapshots; the last-sync stamp shows the exact date and time.
    • Month-end close — a per-client, per-month checklist (categorize, reconcile bank & credit cards, review A/R & A/P, adjusting entries, review P&L, lock) with a progress bar and notes. It shows a live count of uncategorized transactions, quick links to the aging reports, and a reconciliation helper per bank/credit-card account — the QuickBooks book balance as of month-end next to a box for the statement's ending balance, with the difference (✓ when it ties out).
    • Job costing / WIP — cost-to-date and billed-to-date per customer/job (from QuickBooks). Enter each job's contract and estimated cost to see percent complete, earned revenue, and over/(under) billing, with totals — a work-in-progress schedule for construction jobs.
    • Reports → 🔎 For review — transactions sitting in Uncategorized / Ask My Accountant accounts that still need a category; click Categorize → to reclassify one to the right account. Reports → 🧾 1099 vendor tracking — cash paid per vendor this year (credit-card payments excluded), flagging who crossed $600, whether they're marked 1099-eligible, and whether a tax ID / W-9 is on file — a year-end 1099-NEC prep worklist.
    • Tasks — per-client to-dos with assignee, due date, and optional repeat (weekly/monthly/quarterly/yearly); overdue items flag red. Click ✏️ Edit on any task to change its title, due date, repeat, or assignee in place.
    • Time — time on a client is now logged automatically (the manual Time tab / punch clock was removed). While a client workspace is your focused, active window, the seconds accrue and roll up into Admin → Bookkeeping time; it pauses on idle (~5 min), tab switch, or minimize. Admins only — only app-admins are tracked, and only admins see the totals.
    • Files — see below.
    • Email — see below.
    • Certified payroll — create and print Minnesota DLI certified-payroll reports (header, employees with daily ST/OT hours, rates, auto-totaled deductions, Statement of Compliance). Search the report list by job/project, payroll #, or employee. Signatures: the Statement of Compliance can be signed three ways — ✍️ Sign now (draw or type on this device), 📌 Insert saved signature (each company keeps one reusable signature, e.g. the owner’s), or 📨 Send a signing link for signers without app access: they get a private link showing only a signature pad (no payroll data), sign on their phone, and it saves as that company’s signature — you get a notification when it arrives. The signature prints on the signature line of the form. ✨ Auto-fill from a file lets you upload a payroll register or timesheet (PDF, a photo, or Excel/CSV) and AI pre-fills the employees, daily hours, rates and deductions — always review every value before saving (it uses only the last 4 of any SSN and won't invent daily hours when the source shows only weekly totals). Save stores a copy in the client's Files tagged Certified Payroll (so it's kept with the client and re-saving replaces the prior copy); Print MN form opens a print-ready version.
    • Pay applications — create and print progress-billing Application & Certificate for Payment documents in the style of AIA G702/G703: enter the schedule of values (continuation sheet), change orders and retainage %, and the G702 summary (lines 1–9, current payment due) computes live. Next app → starts the following application and carries the schedule of values forward (each line's work-to-date rolls into "from previous"). Save files a copy in the client's Files tagged Pay Application; Print G702/G703 opens a print-ready version. Formatted in the style of AIA G702/G703 — not an official AIA form; verify figures before submitting.
    • Customer info — full details (legal name, contact, EIN, fiscal year, payroll schedule, address, notes); autosaves.
    • Team & access — admins set who can see this client and enable/disable it; others see a read-only note.

    Files — internal vs. the client's drive

    The Files section keeps two clearly-separated areas:

    • 🔒 Internal BCC files — private to Blue Collar Coach (the client can't see them). Upload with categories/tags; search, filter by category, and sort the list, which shows each file's uploader and date.
    • ☁️ Client's shared drive — link the client's own Google Drive or OneDrive using the login they set up for the firm. Once connected (the panel shows the connected account), browse folders, upload, and download the files you share with that client. If the drive shows connected but files won't load (a permissions / 403 error), the Drive scope wasn't granted — click Reconnect and, on the Google consent screen, be sure to check the box that grants access to Drive.

    Click any file to preview it right in the app — images, PDFs, and text open in a viewer, no download needed (Download and Open-in-new-tab are there if you want them).

    ✂️ PDF tools — edit & sign: click ✏️ Edit / Sign on any PDF preview (or the PDF tools button next to the upload) to open the built-in editor. Organize & split: drag pages to reorder, rotate or delete them, extract selected pages to a new PDF, or merge in another PDF. Sign & fill: draw or type a signature, then place it — plus text, date, and check-mark stamps — anywhere on the page (drag to move, corner-drag to resize). Everything happens in your browser; nothing is uploaded until you choose Download, Save to internal files, or Save to shared drive.

    Scanning tip: the AI receipt scanner reads photos (JPG/PNG), screenshots, and PDFs. iPhone HEIC photos can't be read directly — either take a screenshot of the receipt, or set iPhone → Settings → Camera → Formats → “Most Compatible” before shooting, then re-scan.

    Email — a shared mailbox, in-app

    Each client can have a dedicated shared mailbox (e.g. mikesrepair@bluecollarcoach.us): an admin creates the shared mailbox once in Microsoft 365 (free, no license) and enters the address on the client's Email tab. From then on the whole team works that mailbox without opening Outlook:

    • Compose & reply from the client address, with your own signature auto-appended. Open any message to read the full email and reply / reply-all in a pop-up.
    • Read / unread is tracked per teammate, and each message shows who read it and who replied — while still sending from the one client address.
    • Tag, assign to a teammate, and archive messages; filter by All / Unread / Assigned to me / Tagged / Archived, or search.

    Management dashboard

    Managers (admins, or anyone with the bookkeeping "admin" permission) get a Management dashboard toggle on the main page: firm-wide KPIs across all companies, and the place to Connect a company (run the QuickBooks sign-in) and manage access. Per company, admins control Enabled and an Access editor for exactly which users can see it.

    Import tasks from Planner: to seed client to-dos from Microsoft Planner, admins get an 📋 Import tasks from Planner button in the Management dashboard. In Planner, open a plan → ⋯ → Export plan to Excel, then upload the file here. Tasks are matched to clients by their Planner label (the app auto-guesses the client and lets you correct each mapping), previews the per-client counts, and creates the tasks in each client's Tasks tab. A task with two client labels goes to both; unlabeled tasks can be routed to one client or skipped. (A CSV or any spreadsheet with a Title column works too.)

    Going live on a production QuickBooks company: in Admin → Integrations → QuickBooks, set Environment = production and paste your production Client ID/Secret, then Connect the real company. Each company remembers its own environment, so a real company always uses live books even if a sandbox company is still connected.
    CRM companies vs QuickBooks companies: a CRM company is a contact record; a QuickBooks company is a connected accounting file. They're separate — connect QuickBooks here, manage relationships in CRM.

    Training

    Courses + lessons your team or clients work through.

    Each course = title + blurb + ordered list of lessons (title, video URL, duration minutes). Click any course card to edit; rows in the lesson editor have inline "+ Add lesson" / × remove.

    Enrollment + per-lesson progress tracking lives in bcc-enrollment-* docs (one per user × course); the dedicated learner view is on the roadmap.

    Events

    Workshops, meetups, intensives, with attendee bookings.

    Each event card shows when, title, location, and "N / capacity booked" + Open/Closed badge. Click to open the modal: title, start/end datetime, location, capacity, registration open Y/N, description.

    Inside the modal, a Bookings section lets you pick a contact from the dropdown (anyone in your CRM) or paste a free-form name. Each booking is timestamped; × to remove.

    Knowledge Base

    Internal playbooks in Markdown.

    Left pane: searchable article list with tag chips. Right pane: reader. Click + New article to draft; click Edit on any article to revise.

    Markdown supported lightly: # heading, ## sub, **bold**, `code`. Tag filter auto-populates from data.

    Admin Center

    Everything that configures the workspace, in one place. Admins only.

    Users

    Microsoft 365 tenant members pulled via Graph. Promote teammates to admin or mark inactive (the signed-in user is always auto-listed). The ⚙ gear on each row opens that person's per-app permissions — see Roles & permissions.

    Customer Types

    The dropdown source CRM + Engagements read. Add / rename / recolor / delete — saved as a single bcc-customer-types-v1 doc.

    Integrations

    Connector cards for QuickBooks and the marketing channels. Paste credentials and click Test connection; once green, the matching module wires the saved creds in automatically — no env-var trip required. Microsoft Outlook shows as “Connected automatically for all users” — it needs no setup (see Microsoft 365 integration).

    Company Info

    Legal name, display name, phones, email, website, address, tagline. Drives the letterhead on customer-facing artifacts (rate sheet today; contracts + reports next).

    Security

    Rotate the activity-log password and edit the domain allow-list. The password field is masked — leave it blank to keep the current one, or type a new value to change it (toggle Show to reveal what you're typing). The tenant-level Entra restriction is already enforced; this is a belt-and-suspenders layer for guest scenarios.

    Data & Backups

    Storage tally by key prefix + a one-click JSON snapshot export of every bcc-* doc in your browser. Useful before any architectural change.

    Roles & permissions

    Who can see and do what.

    There are two global roles — Admin and Member — plus an optional per-app override on top.

    Default access for a Member

    Out of the box, a non-admin can use: My Day, Sessions, Scheduler, Bookkeeping, Documents, Team Chat, Training, Events, and Knowledge Base. They do not see CRM, Engagements, Marketing, Rate Sheet, or the Admin Center.

    Admins

    Admins have full access to everything, including the Admin Center and the Activity Log.

    Per-person overrides

    In Admin → Users, the ⚙ gear opens a grid where you can set each app to Admin / Edit / View / None for that person — e.g. grant one member CRM access, or restrict another further. Hidden modules disappear from their menu, home tiles, and are blocked if deep-linked. Only what differs from the default is stored.

    Microsoft 365 integration

    Outlook calendar and email, wired for everyone automatically.

    The app talks to Microsoft Graph tenant-wide (admin-consented), so every signed-in @bluecollarcoach.us user gets the integration with no per-person “Connect” step:

    • Calendar: sessions you create/edit/delete mirror to your Outlook calendar, and Pull from Outlook brings events the other way (on Sessions).
    • Email: the app can send mail on your behalf (e.g. Rate Sheet → “Send for review”).
    • Directory: the team/user lists and dropdowns are populated from your Entra tenant.
    This is the standard tenant-wide app integration; access is admin-consented and scoped to your organization.

    How data flows

    A two-sentence model that explains how every page stays in sync.

    Every save in any module writes a bcc-<thing>-<id> key to your browser's localStorage. A hook in bcc-api.js sees the write, debounces 1.2 seconds, and PUTs the doc to /api/data, which upserts into Cosmos under your tenant partition. Reads work the other way: on page load, bcc-api.js mirrors every prefixed doc from Cosmos into localStorage, then every page just reads from localStorage. Offline works. Saves queue and retry every 5 seconds + immediately when the browser comes back online.

    For the schemaless JSON shape of each module's docs, see docs/data-shapes.md in the repo.

    Activity Log

    Who did what, when. Password-gated.

    Every save / delete / sign-in / integration test / chat moderation action fires a structured audit row. The feed filters by category (Admin / CRM / Sessions / Documents / Rates / My Day / Integrations / System) and by user. Sortable by time; clickable to drill in.

    Bookkeeping actions are recorded on the server at the moment they happen — posting to QuickBooks, attaching a file, sending a client email, uploading/deleting a document, and each nightly or manual QuickBooks sync — so the log is complete and accurate even if a browser is closed mid-action. Every row carries a full date-and-time stamp, and client-scoped actions also appear on that client's Activity tab in Bookkeeping.

    Unexpected errors (server or in-browser) are captured to an error log that admins can review at Admin → Errors.

    Access is password-gated; an admin sets and rotates the password from Admin → Security (it's stored masked).

    Keyboard + a11y

    • Every interactive element has a visible focus ring + 44 px touch target.
    • Skip-to-content link at the top of every page (tab to it from the very first focus).
    • Toasts are aria-live; the offline banner is announced on connection drop.
    • Drawer + topbar are reachable from keyboard alone (no mouse-only menus).
