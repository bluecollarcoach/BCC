How to use BCC Connect

One app for the office, the field, drivers, operators, and maintenance. Everything lives in one place so the day’s plan, time, materials, photos, and issues stay in one record per job.

The way it’s wired

The Scheduler is the source of truth for what every crew is doing each day. The My Day view, the T&M sheet, the My Day, and the Job Board all read from the same job list. Issues raised from Inspections, Inspections, or the field all land in one Maintenance Inbox.

⚡Quick start by role

Office / PM
Quote emergency work on the Rate Sheet. Plan the week in the Scheduler. Triage issues in the Maintenance Inbox. Review submitted T&M & daily logs.
Crew lead
Check Today’s Plan in the morning. Run the pre-trip. Send a daily log at end of day. Submit T&M.
Driver
Run the Pre-Trip Inspection before pulling out. Any FAIL routes to maintenance automatically.
Operator
Walk around the unit in Equipment Check. Log parts needed; they go to the supply run list.
Maintenance
Work the inbox. Filter by urgent, assign to a tech, add updates as you fix.
Yard / Office
Keep Inventory current. The Supply Run tab auto-fills with low-stock items + parts requests.

💰Emergency Rate Sheet

Office · Sales Customer-facing

The published 24-hour emergency rate sheet, identical to the Excel document. Use it to quote a customer on an emergency call, capture their signature in the authorization block, and print or save a PDF copy as the contract record.

Open Rate Sheet →

What’s on the sheet

  • Excavators (hourly with operator): Mini $175, Mid-Size PC-88 $195, Mid-Size PC-138 $205, Cat 316 $215.
  • Loaders & Dozer (hourly with operator): Track Skid Loader $165, Front End Loader $215, Dozer $215.
  • Compaction (daily): Jumping Jack $120, Plate Tamper $180, Walk-Behind Drum $235.
  • Attachments (daily): Hydro Hammer $350, Plate Packer $275.
  • Labor (per man-hour): Laborer $135 / Overtime premium +$67.50 / Double-time premium +$135.
  • Other: Mobilization $400/move · Trench Box $340/day · Trucking $150/hr.

Authorizing a customer

  1. Confirm the customer over the phone or on site. Open the rate sheet on the office device or tablet.
  2. Scroll to the Customer Authorization block. Fill in Company name, contact, email, phone, cell, and job location.
  3. Have the customer type their full name in Authorized customer signature. Set the date.
  4. Tap Save Authorization. The record lands on the Authorizations tab.
  5. Tap Print / Save PDF to send a copy to the customer (browser print menu — the page is print-styled for letter size).
Contract terms are listed in full on the rate sheet (admin fees, credit-card surcharge, unmarked-utility liability, materials/subcontractor mark-up, Net-30 terms) along with the Minnesota lien notices. They print with the rate sheet on every page.

Rates change — how do I update them?

Rates are coded directly into rates.html as a constant called RATES near the top of the script block, with an EFFECTIVE_DATE line just below. When the office issues a new pricing sheet, edit those values and bump the date.

▤Scheduler

Office Visible to field

Visual weekly plan. Five crew/unit rows by five day columns. Jobs sit as cards in cells. The field view pulls from the same data, so as soon as you drop a card, the crew sees their assignment.

Open Scheduler →

How to schedule a job

  1. Click + New Job in the top right. The detail panel opens — fill in name, type, customer, location, estimated hours, crew members, equipment, and notes.
  2. The new job lands in the Unscheduled pool on the left.
  3. Drag the card from the pool into the crew/day cell where it should run.
  4. Click an existing card to edit it. Drag back to the pool to un-schedule.
On phone / tablet: long-press a card to select it (it’ll outline in teal), then tap the cell where it should go. Tap again to drop into the pool.

Other controls

  • ← / → step the week. Today snaps back to this week.
  • + Crew adds a new crew/unit row.
  • Job type colors the left border of every card. Hydrovac is sky-blue, Excavation amber, Water/Sewer slate, Fire Hydrant red, Demo dark, Emergency red on pink.

📍Today’s Plan

Field Phone-first

The crew opens this on their phone in the morning. They see what they’re doing today, where they’re going, who they’re with, what equipment is on the job, and any notes from the office.

Open Field view →

How to use it

  1. Pick your name from the dropdown in the top right (it remembers).
  2. The card with today’s job shows up. Tap the location to open Google Maps. Tap a crewmate to start a call.
  3. Tap Clock In when you start, Clock Out when you wrap. Tap Open T&M to fill out the time-and-materials sheet pre-loaded with this job.
  4. Tap ⚠ Report Issue to send a note to maintenance from the field (truck, equipment, site, supply).
No job assigned? Shop day — log shop hours via T&M instead. The Report Issue button still works.

$T&M Sheet

Office Foreman

Replaces the paper / Excel time-and-materials sheet. Three line tables — Labor, Equipment, Materials — auto-totaled with a material markup field, signed and submitted from the field.

Open T&M →

Filling out a sheet

  1. Pick the Job from the dropdown. Customer + notes auto-fill from the scheduler. If you opened T&M via the Field view, the job is already selected.
  2. Set date and foreman.
  3. Labor: + Add row per worker. Enter ST and OT hours. Rates default to $95 / $142.50; override per row if needed.
  4. Equipment: + Add row per unit. Defaults pull from the seeded equipment rate list (hydrovac $285/hr, excavator 320 $235/hr, etc.).
  5. Materials: + Add row per line item. Description, qty, unit, unit price. Subtotal calculates per row.
  6. Watch the bottom totals update live. Adjust material markup % if the contract calls for something other than 15%.
  7. Save draft if you’re mid-shift. Sign & Submit sends it to the office.

The Submitted tab lists everything that’s been turned in. Click Open to reopen any sheet for review or edits.

🚚Pre-Trip Inspection

Driver Auto-routes failures

DOT-style pre-trip checklist on the phone. Categories adapt to the vehicle: hydrovacs add tank/boom items, trailered units add coupling/chains.

Open Pre-Trip →

Running a pre-trip

  1. Pick the vehicle/unit, your name, and the current odometer.
  2. Walk the unit. Mark each item PASS, FAIL, or N/A.
  3. If something fails, a notes field appears under it — describe what’s wrong.
  4. The progress bar fills as you complete items. Submit unlocks when every item is checked.
  5. Hit Submit. Every FAIL automatically posts an urgent ticket to the Maintenance Inbox.
Do not operate the unit if any condition is unsafe, even if the rest of the inspection passes. Call maintenance.

🚛Trucking Slip

Driver Office reviews

The digital version of the paper trucking ticket (Ticket #22044 style). Driver logs every load with start/stop times, tons or yards, and material, then the office gets a saved record with totals and a signature.

Open Trucking Slip →

Filling out a ticket

  1. The ticket number is auto-assigned, counting up from #22045. Edit it if the office wants a specific number.
  2. Pick the driver (defaults to who you are), truck number, and job from the scheduler dropdown. Bill-to and dump-site auto-fill from the selected job.
  3. Fill in loading-site location and dump-site location.
  4. Log Travel to Job start & stop times — interval auto-calculates.
  5. For each load, enter Start, Stop, Tons / Yds, and pick a Material (Class 5, Class 7, Black Dirt, Sand, Pea Rock, Gravel, Concrete spoils, Asphalt millings, Clay, Mud, Topsoil, or Other). Add more load rows with + Add load if needed.
  6. Set Job Start Time and Job Stop Time. Totals (Loads, Haul Time, Travel, Total Hours) refresh as you type.
  7. Type the authorizing signature and Save Slip. Use Print / PDF to send a copy to the customer or office.
Past Tickets tab lists every saved slip with the ticket #, driver, truck, date, and load count. Click Open on any ticket to reopen, edit, or reprint.

🔥Fire Hydrant Inspection

Inspector Office reviews

Digital version of Precision’s paper Fire Hydrant Inspection Form. Same 16 questions in the same order, so the record matches what a fire marshal expects to see.

Open Hydrant Inspection →

Filling out an inspection

  1. Fill the header: Property name, optional Precision job # (picked from the scheduler), property address, Hydrant ID, and Location on property (e.g. “SW corner near parking”). Inspector and date auto-fill from your sign-in and today’s date.
  2. For each of the 16 inspection items, tap Yes, No, or N/A. If you tap No, a comments box opens on that row — describe what was found.
  3. For a clean inspection, hit Mark all Yes at the top to fill empty rows fast. It won’t overwrite an answer you’ve already set.
  4. Type your full name into Inspector signature at the bottom (defaults to your name; change it if a different inspector is signing).
  5. Tap Save inspection. GPS is captured automatically so the record proves where the inspection happened.
Past Inspections tab lists every saved inspection with the property, hydrant ID, inspector, date, GPS chip, and a CLEAR / N-fail badge. Tap any row to open a printable detail view — the layout matches the paper form so you can hand a fire marshal a clean PDF.

⚙Equipment Check

Operator Auto-routes failures Posts to supply list

Walk-around inspection for field equipment — hydrovac, excavator, skid steer, compactor, generator, pump, light tower. The checklist adapts to the equipment type.

Open Equipment →

How it works

  1. Tap the equipment card up top. Enter operator + hour-meter reading.
  2. Run the General checklist (fluids, leaks, guards) then the type-specific list.
  3. In Parts / Supplies Needed, type any consumables or parts the unit needs (e.g. "2 ea 3/8" hose 24"") and hit Add.
  4. Submit. Fails go to maintenance as urgent. Each parts request becomes a supply-list ticket that shows up in Inventory › Supply Run.

📦Inventory

Yard / Office Everyone reads

Parts, consumables, PPE, fittings, fluids. Quantities per truck or yard. Two tabs:

Open Inventory →

Stock tab

  • Search filters live. Each row shows on-hand qty, location, status (OK, LOW, OUT), and reorder threshold.
  • Use + / − buttons (or type) to adjust quantity as you use or restock items.
  • Click ⚙ on a row to edit name, category, location, unit, qty, or reorder threshold.
  • Hit + Add item to add a new inventory line.

Supply Run tab

Replaces the “Groceries” app. The list auto-fills from two sources:

  • Low-stock items (qty ≤ reorder threshold) — tagged AUTO.
  • Parts requests from Equipment Check or field issue reports — tagged MAINT.

You can also type items directly into the “Add to list” box. At the supply house, tap each item’s checkbox as you pick it up; checked items move to a Picked up section at the bottom.

🔧Maintenance Inbox

Maintenance Office

Every truck issue, equipment fault, site safety note, and parts request ends up here. One funnel. The top four stat tiles tell you urgency at a glance.

Open Inbox →

Where tickets come from

  • Pre-Trip Inspection — one ticket per FAIL, marked urgent, category truck.
  • Equipment Check — one ticket per FAIL (urgent) and one per parts request (normal, supply).
  • Field > Report Issue — manual ticket from the crew, any category.

Working a ticket

  1. Filter to Open or Urgent at the top.
  2. Click a ticket card. The side panel shows the full report and reporter.
  3. Change Status (Open → In Progress → Closed), pick a Severity, Assign to a tech.
  4. Add an update in the Add update box (what you found, parts ordered, ETA). Saved updates show in the Activity timeline.
  5. Mark Closed when fixed.

📋Job Board

Everyone PM view

Replaces the Trello board. Six columns matching the existing workflow:

Open Job Board →
  1. New / Inbound — just-arrived RFPs, customer calls, emergency-rate authorizations.
  2. Locates Called In? — waiting on Gopher One / utility locates before we can dig.
  3. Scheduled Jobs — on the calendar, crew assigned, ready to roll.
  4. Jobs Today — active right now, crew on site.
  5. Completed — Waiting on Resto / Traffic Control — work’s done but post-work pending.
  6. Needs Invoiced — ready for billing.

How it relates to the Scheduler

  • Job Board and Scheduler share the same jobs — two views, one data set.
  • A job’s column comes from its status. Not assigned = New. Assigned to a crew on a future date = Scheduled. Assigned for today = Jobs Today automatically. Other columns you set explicitly by dragging.
  • Dragging a card back to New / Inbound clears the crew/day.
  • Dragging to Jobs Today auto-sets the day to today if it wasn’t already.

What’s on each card

Click any card to open the side panel. Every card now supports:

  • Address (primary title), work description, customer, type, hours, crew members.
  • Contacts — one or more contact people per job: name, role, company, email, phone. The card shows a contact-count badge.
  • Checklist — per-job items like “Locates called”, “Permit pulled”, “Materials ordered”. Card shows n/m done; turns green when complete.
  • Created From Email — paste an RFP’s subject and sender to attribute the job. Card shows an envelope icon.
  • Photos — camera on mobile, library on desktop. Stored full-size as data URLs (5MB origin cap, see Storage below).
  • Files — PDFs, docs, anything. Color-coded by extension (PDF red, image pink, other blue). Click the download arrow to save.
  • Activity — running comment thread per job; stamps each note with date and submitter.
Use the search box + type filter at the top to focus the board.

📷Daily Log

Field Office reviews

End-of-day write-up per job. Captures weather, hours, crew on site, work performed, materials, delays, and photos. Pinned to the job for the office to review.

Open Daily Log →

Filling one out

  2. Tap the weather chip that fits (sunny, rain, snow, etc.) and enter the temp.

⚙Admin & Settings

Admin only

One settings page that controls users, roles, rates, equipment, crews, lists, and company info. Changes flow into the rest of the app live — the rate sheet, T&M defaults, equipment lists, and crew rows all read from here when present.

Open Admin →

Tabs

  • Users — the 22 team members with role, status, email, phone. Lock icon opens per-user permission overrides on top of the role’s defaults.
  • Roles & Permissions — seven seeded roles (Admin, Office/PM, Crew Lead, Crew, Driver, Maintenance, Yard). Each row of checkboxes maps a role to its permissions across Admin / Operations / Field / Yard / Maintenance / Reports.
  • Rates — T&M defaults (ST/OT/DT labor rates, material markup %, equipment $/hr table) and the full emergency rate sheet (excavators, loaders, compaction, attachments, labor, other). Effective date is editable. Saved values override the hardcoded defaults in rates.html and tm.html on next page load.
  • Equipment & Vehicles — manage the equipment list (for the equipment check) and the vehicle list (for pre-trip).
  • Crews — add or rename crews, set the lead, multi-select members. Crew rows show in the scheduler.
  • Lists — materials (used in the trucking slip dropdown) and inventory locations (Shop, Yard, per-truck…).
  • Company — legal name, address, phone, emergency phone, primary contact, office email, website, tagline, and the trucking ticket counter.
Save / Discard bar sticks to the bottom of every tab. Edits don’t persist until you click Save changes. Export config in the sidebar downloads the full settings JSON for backup.
v0.1 enforcement: per-user permissions are captured but not yet enforced page-by-page — that gating comes with the v0.2 backend.

📍Location tracking

Everyone

On every record-creating action, the app asks the browser for GPS coordinates and attaches them to the saved record. Captured silently in the background, so the UI doesn’t wait. If the user denies the prompt or location is unavailable, the record still saves — just without a pin.

Where GPS is captured

  • Clock In / Clock Out on the Field view.
  • Report Issue from Field — the resulting maintenance ticket gets the field crew’s location.
  • T&M sheet at draft save and submit.
  • Pre-Trip Inspection at submit — the record and every auto-created maintenance ticket get the driver’s location.
  • Equipment Check at submit — record + tickets get GPS.
  • Daily Log at submit.
  • Trucking Slip at save.

Where to see pins

Anywhere a record with GPS shows up, a small purple pin chip with lat/lng appears. Tap it to open Google Maps. Pins show on:

  • Maintenance Inbox ticket cards (in the meta line).
  • T&M Submitted list rows.
  • Daily Log past entries.
  • Trucking Slip past tickets.
  • Field view today’s job card (next to CLOCKED IN).
Permissions: the browser only asks for location once per origin. If a user denies it, future records save without GPS — tell them to re-grant permission via the browser’s site settings if you need accurate tracking. iOS Safari is the strictest about this.

What’s stored

Each location object on a record looks like { lat, lng, accuracy, ts }. accuracy is the radius in meters reported by the browser (Wi-Fi positioning is typically 20–100m, GPS on a phone is 5–20m). ts is the capture time. Nothing leaves the device; storage is local in localStorage with the rest of the record.

☁Cloud, Auth & SSO

v0.2 (Azure-hosted)

When the app is deployed to Azure Static Web Apps (see “Deploying to Azure” below), three things change vs. the local v0.1 demo:

  1. Single sign-on via Entra ID (Azure AD). Every page except the 403 redirect requires a signed-in user. Sign-in is handled by the Static Web Apps platform at /.auth/login/aad — no auth code in the app itself. The user’s identity is auto-mapped to the bcc-field-who name picker on first load, so the field / chat / daily-log views know who you are without asking.
  2. Data lives in Cosmos DB (Free Tier — 1000 RU/s, 25 GB, $0/mo forever). The same bcc-* keys you saw locally now exist as documents in one container, partitioned by tenant. All users in the company share the same dataset.
  3. Cross-device sync is transparent. The page code still reads/writes localStorage exactly like it did in v0.1; bcc-api.js hooks Storage.prototype.setItem and debounces writes to /api/data. On page load it pulls every bcc-* doc from the server and mirrors them into localStorage. Result: the Office can drag a job on a desktop and the field crew sees it on their phone seconds later.

The sync chip

Top-right of every page shows a small chip with the signed-in user’s name and a status dot:

  • Green dot — signed in, cloud sync active.
  • Amber dot, pulsing — pushing a batch of changes right now.
  • Red dot — push failed; will retry in 5 seconds. Click Sign out and back in if it persists.
  • Grey dot — anonymous / running locally only (no cloud sync).

Sign-out link clears your session and redirects to the home page. Sign-in goes through Entra ID with whatever MFA / conditional-access policies you’ve set on the tenant.

Roles vs. permissions

Two layers, intentionally:

  • Static Web Apps roles are the platform-level gate. The administrator role guards /admin.html at the routing layer. Anyone else trying to load it gets the 403 page. Assign via Azure Portal → Static Web App → Role management.
  • App-level permissions live in the admin config (Users tab, Roles & Permissions tab). Those are read by the app to decide what to render — e.g. show the “Mark Invoiced” button only to users with invoice_jobs. v0.2 captures them but doesn’t yet gate every page; v0.3 will plumb the checks server-side via the Functions API.

Costs

For the team’s expected scale: $0/month. SWA Free covers 100 GB bandwidth, Functions executions are within the free quota, Cosmos Free Tier is forever. Custom domain SSL is also free.

🚀Deploying to Azure

Admin only

Full step-by-step runbook is in DEPLOY.md in the project root. Summary:

  1. Push the repo to GitHub (the SWA Free SKU only deploys from GitHub).
  2. Create a GitHub personal access token (repo + workflow scopes).
  3. Register an Entra ID app: az ad app create, capture CLIENT_ID + secret + tenant GUID.
  4. Run infra/deploy.ps1 or infra/deploy.sh. Provisions Cosmos DB (Free Tier), the Static Web App, and wires all app settings (COSMOS_*, AZURE_*, BCC_TENANT_ID).
  5. Update the Entra app’s redirect URI to point at your new SWA hostname.
  6. Replace AZURE_TENANT_ID in staticwebapp.config.json with the real GUID and push — GitHub Actions deploys.
  7. Invite admin users via the SWA portal’s Role management panel.
  8. (Optional) add the custom domain app.bluecollarcoach.us.
One-Cosmos-free-tier limit: only one Cosmos account per Azure subscription can have Free Tier enabled. If that’s already used, pass -EnableCosmosFreeTier $false — the minimum non-free Cosmos costs ~$18/month.
The deployment token is only visible in the SWA portal after the SWA is created. Copy it from Manage deployment token into your GitHub repo’s secrets as AZURE_STATIC_WEB_APPS_API_TOKEN, then re-run the GitHub Action.

💾Storage & limitations

This is the v0.1 private preview. All data lives in the browser’s localStorage, on the device you’re using. Two consequences:

  • No sync between devices yet. If you create a job on the office computer, it won’t automatically show up on a phone — that comes with the backend in v0.2.
  • Storage cap. Browsers give each site roughly 5 MB. Photos are stored as base64, which is bigger than the original file. Plan on a few dozen full-size photos per browser before things tighten up. The production version will store photos in cloud storage.

❓FAQ

How do I switch who I am?

In My Day, Chat, and My Day, there’s a name picker in the top right. It remembers across pages.

I assigned a job but nobody’s on the crew.

You can have a job assigned to a crew unit (e.g. Hydrovac 1) without explicit individuals. To show it under a person’s Today’s Plan, open the job and check the names on the Crew members list.

How do I report an issue if I don’t have an assigned job?

Open Today’s Plan with your name selected. Even on a no-job day, the Report Issue button is available.

Where do I print a T&M sheet for the customer?

v0.1 doesn’t have PDF export yet — use the browser’s Print menu on the T&M page (Ctrl/Cmd+P). PDF export is on the roadmap.

How do I make a brand-new crew on the fly?

On the Scheduler, + Crew in the toolbar prompts for a name and lead. Members can be assigned by opening any job and picking from the crew chips.
