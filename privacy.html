Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 27, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Blue Collar Coach (“Blue Collar Coach,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects information in connection with BCC Connect (the “Service”), our internal business-management application available at connect.bluecollarcoach.us.

BCC Connect is a private application provided to authorized members of Blue Collar Coach and its participating clients. Access requires an invited account; the Service is not offered to the general public.

1. Information we collect

We collect only the information needed to operate the Service for you:

Account & identity

When you sign in, we authenticate you through Microsoft Entra ID (single sign-on). We receive your name, work email address, and basic directory profile. We do not see or store your password.

Business and client records you provide

Information you enter into the Service — such as contacts, companies, engagements, tasks, notes, time entries, documents, certified-payroll records, and customer details.

Financial data from connected accounting systems

If you connect an Intuit QuickBooks Online company, we access financial records (for example accounts, transactions, customers, vendors, invoices, bills, and reports) to provide bookkeeping and reporting features. See Section 4.

Email, calendar, and files from connected accounts

If you connect a Microsoft 365 mailbox/calendar or a Google Drive or Microsoft OneDrive account, we access messages, calendar events, and files as needed to provide the related features. See Sections 5 and 6.

Usage, device & log data

We record limited operational data such as request logs, timestamps, IP address, browser/device information, in-app activity (audit) events, and — if you opt in — push-notification tokens, so we can run, secure, and troubleshoot the Service.

2. How we use information

  • Provide, maintain, and improve the Service and its features.
  • Authenticate users and enforce role- and client-based access controls.
  • Sync, display, and let authorized users work with your connected data (accounting, email, calendar, files).
  • Send in-app and push notifications you have enabled.
  • Secure the Service, prevent abuse, debug, and maintain audit trails.
  • Comply with legal obligations.

We do not sell your information, and we do not use it for advertising.

3. Connected services & sub-processors

We rely on a small number of trusted providers to operate the Service. They process data only as needed to provide their service to us:

  • Microsoft Azure — cloud hosting, database, and file storage.
  • Microsoft Entra ID & Microsoft Graph (Microsoft 365) — sign-in, and (if connected) mail, calendar, and OneDrive.
  • Intuit QuickBooks Online — accounting data for connected companies.
  • Google LLC (Google Drive API) — access to a connected client’s Google Drive.
  • Anthropic — AI extraction of data from receipt/invoice images you scan (see Section 7).

4. Intuit QuickBooks data

When you connect a QuickBooks Online company, you authorize the connection through Intuit’s OAuth 2.0 sign-in; we never receive your Intuit password.

  • What we access: the QuickBooks data needed for bookkeeping and reporting — accounts, transactions, customers, vendors, items, invoices, bills, payments, and financial reports for the company you connect.
  • How we use it: solely to provide the Service’s bookkeeping, reporting, and transaction features to authorized users of your firm. We do not use QuickBooks data for advertising and we do not sell it.
  • How we store it: we store the OAuth access/refresh tokens and a limited amount of QuickBooks data (such as cached monthly financial summaries) securely on Microsoft Azure to provide the features above.
  • How we share it: only with the sub-processors in Section 3 that host or operate the Service, and only as required by law. We do not share QuickBooks data with third parties for their own purposes.
  • Disconnecting: you can disconnect a QuickBooks company at any time, which removes our stored tokens and ends our access. You may also revoke access from your Intuit account at account.intuit.com.

BCC Connect is an independent application. It is not provided, endorsed, or certified by Intuit, and “QuickBooks” is a trademark of Intuit Inc.

5. Google user data

If you connect a client’s Google Drive, you authorize the connection through Google’s OAuth 2.0 sign-in. We access Google Drive files only to let authorized users browse, open, upload, and download the files shared between your firm and that client.

BCC Connect’s use and transfer of information received from Google APIs to any other app will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Specifically, data obtained through Google APIs is used only to provide the file features described above. We do not use it for advertising, do not sell it, do not use it to train generalized artificial-intelligence or machine-learning models, and do not allow humans to read it except where (a) you have given consent for specific files, (b) it is necessary for security purposes such as investigating abuse, or (c) it is required by law. You can revoke our access at any time in the app or at myaccount.google.com/permissions.

6. Microsoft 365 data

Sign-in is provided by Microsoft Entra ID. If you connect a Microsoft 365 mailbox/calendar or a OneDrive account, we use Microsoft Graph — with your authorization — to send and read messages for a client’s dedicated shared mailbox, sync calendar events, and browse OneDrive files. Microsoft 365 data is used only to provide these features and is handled the same way as described throughout this policy. You can revoke access at myaccount.microsoft.com.

7. AI document processing

When you use the “Scan receipt” feature, the receipt or invoice image you provide is sent to Anthropic’s API to extract details (such as vendor, date, line items, and totals) so the app can pre-fill a transaction. Per Anthropic’s commercial terms, content submitted through the API is not used to train its models. We send only the document you choose to scan.

8. How we share information

We share information only:

  • with the sub-processors listed in Section 3, to operate the Service;
  • among authorized users within your organization, according to the access controls configured for your account and clients;
  • when required by law, regulation, legal process, or to protect rights, safety, and security; and
  • in connection with a business transfer (e.g., merger or acquisition), subject to this policy.

We never sell personal information or connected-account data.

9. Data security

The Service is hosted on Microsoft Azure. We protect information using measures including encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS), encryption at rest for stored data, single sign-on with domain restriction, role- and client-based access controls, server-side storage of OAuth tokens (never exposed to the browser), and audit logging. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, but we work to protect your information and review our safeguards regularly.

10. Data retention

We retain information for as long as needed to provide the Service and for legitimate business or legal purposes. When you disconnect an integration, we delete the associated access tokens and end further access. You may request deletion of your data as described below, subject to legal retention requirements.

11. Your choices & rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, export, or delete your personal information, and to object to or restrict certain processing. To exercise these rights, contact us at the address in Section 15. We will respond consistent with applicable law.

12. Disconnecting integrations

  • In the app: use the relevant Disconnect / Remove control (for a QuickBooks company, a connected drive, or a client mailbox).
  • QuickBooks: account.intuit.com → Apps / Connected apps.
  • Google: myaccount.google.com/permissions.
  • Microsoft: myaccount.microsoft.com.

13. Children’s privacy

The Service is intended for business use by adults and is not directed to children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

14. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the “Last updated” date above and, where appropriate, provide additional notice. Continued use of the Service after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
